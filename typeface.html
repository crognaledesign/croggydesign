VOXL

I have spent a good seventy-five percent of my life obsessing over and learning more about gaming and the gaming industry from business to competition. Aside from art and design, gaming has been my longest lived passion, so when an opportunity arose to design a typeface, I knew exactly what I wanted it to look like, and what the end result would be.

//process

VOXL

VOXL is my own original typeface. While ideating and conceptualizing the theme of the typeface, I wanted to put a large focus on the gaming and esports industry. When you think of sports teams and organizations, they are all synonomous with the very tall, semi-condensed, blocky typefaces. Gaming has always been a little different in terms of visual identity, so I wanted to stick with a blocky look, but make it a bit wider, so as to still fit the fun nature of video games.

//mockups and use-cases

To round out the typeface, I designed a brand to go along with it, as a way to contextualize the use of this typeface.

THE BRAND; PLOT ARMOR

When creating a fake brand, I stuck with the theme of gaming and esports, and attempted to create a fake esports organization to contextualize the use of an esports-oriented typeface.

Esports is still a new market, so there's no real 'rules' that determine what it should or shouldn't look like. I decided to try a different approach to the mainstream esports look.

In doing so, I created a bright and playful colour scheme as well as a logo that wasn't too flat or serious—the crown logo present in the branding materials—all the while attempting to make a cohesive brand while using colour and layout to eccentuate the typeface.

The couple of examples present to here are the two examples that eccentuate the typeface the best.
