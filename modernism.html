Modernism on Paper

Modernism on Paper is a motion piece based on a book cover for a book of the same name, Modernism on Paper.

I wanted to use this project as an opportunity not only to create an outstanding assignment, but also to practice and develop my After Effects skills, specifically through use of After Effects' built in 3D Camera.

//process

Modernism on Paper

Created by Japanese designer, Kawabota Naomiti, this 'poster' was actually designed as the cover of the book titled Modernism on Paper.

When deciding on a design to use, we were given the freedom to choose either a traditional poster, media poster, or in my case, a book cover or promotional material. The goal was to deconstruct the poster and create an animated motion graphic using those specific elements as opposed to creating a totally original concept in an attempt for us, as students, to open our mind even when given a semi-limiting brief.

//storyboard

PRE-PRODUCTION

Video isn't easy; in fact, video tends to be one of the mediums where the majority of the work is done before the editor or designer even touches the timeline.

The storyboard for this video was the one piece that took me the longest. I cycled through a number of ideas in my head, and compared to what ended up on the page, even the final video had changed once it hit the timeline.

Certain shots didn't line up the way I had hoped they would, or lacked a certain energy that I was after. Compared to the rest of the video, those shots seemed bland and because of that, that some creative liberties were taken on the timeline when things began to fall apart during the planning process.

Put simply, video takes a while, and when you're experimenting with different styles, it's hard to determine what will and won't work before you hit the timeline. Once you're in the middle of making things move, it becomes much easier to determine the final look of the project, and I learned that while working on this project.
