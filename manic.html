MANIC

Manic was an animated piece meant to detail and visualize the characteristics of mania using only typography. no symbolism or imagery was allowed for the duration of this project.

In what ways can you manipulate type to visualize and express a word, phrase or feeling without using images or graphics?

//preliminary sketchess

Manic

Relating to or affected by mania.
"The manic interludes the depression."

Showing wild, apparently deranged, excitement and energy.
"A manic grin."

Crazy can be good sometimes—based on the definition of Manic, as part of a type-oriented motion project, the goal was to visually represent the inner turmoil expressed in the above definitions.

PRE-PRODUCTION

For this project, a significantly smaller, yet just as impactful, project as any of the rest, with regards to motion, shows only one thing. When dealing with motion or in the realm of motion design and animation, sometimes, the content is not the important part, but rather, how you showcase that information that makes the difference. .

//moodboard

The initial moodboards for the project aren't necessarily the neatest or tidiest, but I tend to organize myself very sporadically. When creating this initial moodboard, I downloaded and pasted nearly 40 different type styles on a document—I ended up having to narrow down the ones I chose from 40 to this collection of 6. The scope was too large that even to start such a small project, it became to overwhelming, that I didn't even know where to start.
