Manic

Manic

Relating to or affected by Mania
"the manic interludes the depression"

Showing wild, apparently deranged, excitement and energy
"a manic grin"

Based on the definition of Manic, as part of an experimental type oriented motion project, I wanted to try to visually represent the inner turmoil expressed in the above definitions. In two versions of the same piece, I have explored two totally different visualization methods of the word Manic.

Tools
Pencil and Paper
Adobe Illustrator
Adobe After Effects

Process
Initial experimentation for this project began in After Effects. Once a style and visual language were decided upon, the storyboards were created. I created the storyboards digitally using ProCreate on the iPad.

From there, I made a list of characters, colours & visual styles and made notes regarding their tonal differences to help me emulate a future-grunge style.

Initial Versions

Versions 1 & 2, although they were the first renders, of the bunch, are the ones that I feel don't do a great job at representing Mania. I think that might be in part due to a lack of movements that imply speed or agression. The transitions are quick, but they don't have the same energy that something a little bit more grunge-y might have.

The final version was created to combat and circumvent this feeling of being composed, as keeping your composure is the opposite of becoming manic.
