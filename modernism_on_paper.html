Problem
Modernism on Paper is a motion piece based on a book cover for a book of the same name, Modernism on Paper, designed by Japanese designer, Kawabota Naomiti. A book cover, of course, is a static medium. The book cover itself has no movement, so creating a motion piece using strictly the elements present on the book cover was quite the challenge.

Storyboard

Pre-Production
Video isn't easy; in fact, video tends to be one of the mediums where the majority of the work is done before the editor or designer even touches the timeline. The storyboard for this video was the one piece that took me the longest. I cycled through a number of ideas in my head, and compared to what ended up on the page, even the final video had changed once it hit the timeline.

Certain shots didn't line up the way I had hoped they would, or lacked a certain energy that I was after. Compared to the rest of the video, those shots seemed bland and because of that, that some creative liberties were taken on the timeline when things began to fall apart during the planning process.

Put simply, video takes time, and when you're experimenting with different styles, it's hard to determine what will and won't work before you hit the timeline. Once you're in the middle of making things move, it becomes much easier to determine the final look of the project, and I learned that while working on this project.
